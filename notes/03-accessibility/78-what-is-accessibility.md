# What Is Accessibility?

<aside>
✨

**Big idea:** **Accessibility (a11y)** is the practice of designing and building digital products that **everyone** can use — including people with disabilities. It's both a moral imperative and, in many countries, a legal one. Good accessibility benefits *every* user, not just those with disabilities.

</aside>

## ♿ What Is Web Accessibility?

- **Accessibility** (often shortened to **a11y** — the *a*, 11 letters, *y*) means making web content **usable by everyone**, regardless of ability, device, or context.
- Covers visual, auditory, motor, cognitive, and neurological differences.
- An accessible page can be **perceived**, **operated**, **understood**, and **robustly interpreted** by any user agent (browser, screen reader, voice control).

## 🧩 Who Benefits?

Accessibility isn't only for users with permanent disabilities. It also helps:

- People with **temporary impairments** (broken arm, eye strain).
- People in **situational constraints** (bright sunlight, noisy café, slow internet, one-handed phone use).
- **Older adults** with declining vision, hearing, or motor control.
- **All users** — captions help in noisy places, keyboard navigation helps power users, clear language helps non-native speakers.

## 🌐 The POUR Principles (WCAG)

The **Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG)** are built around four principles. Content must be:

| Principle | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **P**erceivable | Users can sense the content (text alternatives, captions, sufficient contrast) |
| **O**perable | UI works with keyboard, voice, switch, touch — not mouse-only |
| **U**nderstandable | Text and behavior are predictable and clear |
| **R**obust | Code works with current and future assistive tech |

WCAG defines three conformance levels: **A** (minimum), **AA** (industry standard), **AAA** (gold standard).

## 💼 Why It Matters

- **Inclusion** — over a billion people worldwide have some form of disability.
- **Legal** — laws like the **ADA** (US), **AODA** (Canada), **EAA** (EU), and Section 508 require accessible digital experiences.
- **SEO** — semantic, well-structured HTML helps both screen readers and search engines.
- **Business** — accessible sites have larger reach, lower bounce rates, and stronger brand reputation.

## ✅ Quick Wins You Can Do Today

- Use **semantic HTML** (`<nav>`, `<main>`, `<button>`, `<h1>`–`<h6>`).
- Provide **`alt` text** on every meaningful image.
- Pair every form input with a **`<label>`**.
- Maintain **sufficient color contrast** (4.5:1 for normal text).
- Make sure your site is **keyboard navigable** — try tabbing through it.
- Don't rely on **color alone** to convey meaning.

## ✅ Key Takeaways

- **Accessibility (a11y)** = making the web usable by everyone.
- Guided by **WCAG**'s POUR principles: **Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust**.
- Helps users with disabilities **and** users in temporary or situational contexts.
- Required by law in many jurisdictions (ADA, EAA, AODA, Section 508).
- Start with **semantic HTML**, **alt text**, **labels**, **contrast**, and **keyboard support**.
